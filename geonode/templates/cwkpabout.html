Advancing a Nexus Approach to the sustainable management of environmental resources

Dear Sir or Madam,

We would like to thank you very much for your interest in our project. Currently, we are implementing a global online database and map application about Constructed Wetlands. With the next information, we would like to provide an overview to you and explain what this project is about.


Globally, human and environmental health is endangered in many areas. In 2015, approx. 2.4 billion people were still using unimproved sanitation facilities. Moreover, sources estimate that around 80% of all wastewater worldwide is released untreated into the environment, leading to deteriorating water quality. The international community seeks to achieve universal access to adequate and equitable sanitation as well as to improve water quality by halving the proportion of untreated wastewater and increasing its safe reuse (targets 6.2 and 6.3 of the Agenda 2030). Constructed Wetlands (CWs) are nature-based solutions which can contribute to achieve the sustainable development goals, but it remains unclear to which extent, as, currently, there is no knowledge about the contribution of constructed wetland systems, e.g. to the global amount of treated wastewater. Moreover, several studies claimed limited data availability in the recent past.


The United Nations University Institute for Integrated Management of Material Fluxes and of Resources (UNU-FLORES) likes to create one central platform about Constructed Wetlands worldwide, consisting of a comprehensive database, including system properties and performance parameters of CWs, and a visual interface representing their geographic distribution through a WebGIS. Additional features may (eventually) include data manipulation, upload and download functions. The aim is to assess the amount of wastewater that is treated globally by constructed wetland systems. Moreover, UNU-FLORES wishes to support scientists, representatives of international organizations, implementers, funding bodies, further experts and the public on a long-term basis, e.g. in CW research, policy-making, financing, implementation, and operation.


To shape this complex web solution according to the needs of its potential users, UNU-FLORES rely on expertsto:

• Take part in a short preliminary survey on the essential elements of the platform and,

• Provide feedback and data during the operation of CWetlands.


The project undertook a comprehensive review of existing databases and literature about Constructed Wetlands, to gain an overview of available data which might be useful for suggesting potential database variables to stakeholders. This comprehensive review included a special emphasis on sludge drying reed beds. A short preliminary survey to the experts and a stakeholder survey provided essential elements for the design of the platform.

After implementation, cooperating experts will have the chance of being the very first users of the database and WebGIS for their own purposes. If any system improvement seems feasible, we would like to receive your feedback again.

All in all, through this online information system, UNU-FLORES aims to contribute to the development of integrated strategies for the sustainable development of environmental resources, such as water, soil, and waste.
